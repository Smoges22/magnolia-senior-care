Accessibility Statement

Magnolia Senior Care aims to make website information clear, readable, mobile-friendly, and easier for families to use during important care decisions.

Last Updated: May 21, 2026

Need help?

If something on this website is difficult to access, Magnolia welcomes feedback and will make reasonable efforts to help.

Contact Magnolia

Our commitment

Magnolia Senior Care wants families, residents, referral partners, and care professionals to be able to use this website with clarity and confidence. Accessibility is part of making healthcare information feel more respectful and less overwhelming.

Design and readability efforts

The website is designed with responsive layouts, readable type, clear navigation, meaningful headings, descriptive links, mobile-friendly sections, and contrast-conscious Magnolia branding.

Ongoing improvements

Magnolia expects the website to continue improving over time as content, Resource Center articles, photos, and technical features are updated.

Third-party tools

Some content or features may rely on third-party services such as maps, social media, video platforms, hosting tools, or form tools. Magnolia does not control every part of those external services, but we aim to keep the website experience as accessible as reasonably possible.

Contact for accessibility concerns

If you have trouble accessing information on this website, please contact Magnolia Senior Care and describe the page, feature, or content that was difficult to use. We will make reasonable efforts to respond and help you get the information you need.
